## Teaching Philosophy

I believe in **learning by doing**. My courses are:

**Project-Based:**
- Students build real systems, not just solve textbook problems
- Final projects often become portfolio pieces
- Emphasis on deployment and production-ready code

**Research-Informed:**
- I bring my latest PhD research into the classroom
- Students read and discuss recent papers
- Bridge between academia and industry practice

**Collaborative:**
- Group projects mirror real-world teamwork
- Peer code reviews and feedback
- Students learn from each other's approaches

**Practical & Ethical:**
- Focus on tools students will actually use (LangChain, FastAPI, PyTorch)
- Discuss ethical implications of AI systems
- Teach responsible AI development

## FAQ (Educator Perspective)

**Q: What do you teach?**  
A: I teach **Intelligent Agents**, **Machine Learning**, and **Game AI Architectures** at İstinye University's Digital Game Design department. My courses focus on practical, project-based learning - students build real AI systems they can showcase in their portfolios.

**Q: What makes your teaching approach different?**  
A: I emphasize **learning by building**. Instead of just theory, students create production-ready systems. I also bring my **research and industry experience** into the classroom - we use tools like LangChain, FastAPI, and Docker, and discuss recent papers.

**Q: What background do students need for your courses?**  
A: For **Machine Learning**: Basic Python and some math (linear algebra, calculus, probability). For **Intelligent Agents**: Prior ML knowledge helps but not required - we start from agent basics. For **Game AI**: Programming experience and interest in games.

**Q: What tools and technologies do you teach?**  
A: **Languages:** Python (primary). **ML/DL:** PyTorch, TensorFlow, Scikit-Learn. **Agent Frameworks:** LangChain, LangGraph, AutoGen. **Deployment:** FastAPI, Docker. **Monitoring:** LangSmith. **Game Engines:** Unity (C# scripting), Unreal (Blueprints + C++).

**Q: Do you mentor students outside of class?**  
A: Absolutely! I supervise:
- Capstone projects and graduation theses
- Research collaborations (I've co-authored with students)
- Career guidance and portfolio reviews
- Open-source contributions

**Q: What do you expect from students?**  
A: **Curiosity** over perfection. I want students who ask "why" and "what if." It's okay to fail - that's how we learn. What matters is:
- Trying new approaches
- Asking for help when stuck
- Sharing knowledge with peers
- Building things you're proud of

**Q: Can non-students take your courses?**  
A: Currently, I teach at İstinye University, but I'm open to:

- Corporate training workshops
- Online course collaborations
- Open-source educational content

**Q: What advice do you give students entering AI?**  
A: Three pieces of advice:

1. **Build projects, not just take courses.** Employers want to see what you can create.
2. **Learn the fundamentals deeply.** Frameworks change, but principles endure.
3. **Stay curious and ethical.** AI is powerful - use it responsibly.

And remember: everyone starts as a beginner. Even I'm constantly learning!
